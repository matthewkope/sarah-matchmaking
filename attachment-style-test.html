Attachment Style Assessment

Understand how you relate in close relationships

This 24-question assessment measures two dimensions of adult attachment — attachment anxiety (worry about closeness and abandonment) and attachment avoidance (discomfort with intimacy and dependence) — and maps you to one of four attachment styles:

  • Secure — comfortable with closeness and independence
  • Anxious-Preoccupied — craves closeness, fears abandonment
  • Dismissive-Avoidant — values independence, uneasy with intimacy
  • Fearful-Avoidant — wants closeness but finds it hard to trust

Answer based on how you generally feel in close or romantic relationships — there are no right or wrong answers. It takes about 5 minutes.

This is an educational self-reflection tool inspired by the research-based two-dimension model of adult attachment (Brennan, Clark & Shaver, 1998). It is not a clinical diagnosis. If relationship distress is significantly affecting your life, consider speaking with a licensed therapist.
